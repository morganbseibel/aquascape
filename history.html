History

      People have been putting aquatic plants and fish together in aquariums forever. However, aquascaping is a relatively new art. There are a few popular styles of planted tanks and the oldest, the Dutch style, emerged in the 1930s. The Dutch style design rarely uses hardscape materials like rocks or driftwood. Dutch style aquascapes are packed with a variety of colorful plants, showing almost no substrate.

      Other popular design styles include the Japanese Nature Aquarium style, Japanese Iwagumi style and the Jungle style. The Nature Aquarium style was developed by aqua designer Takashi Amano in the 1990s. Aquascapes with this style mimic natural landscapes, have a focus point and are often minimalist. They use hardscape materials and less colors than the Dutch style. In the Iwagumi style, rocks dominate the aquascape and low-growing plants are usually used. In the Jungle style, there is dense plant growth resembling a jungle. Hardscape materials are rarely used and plants are meant to densely fill this aquascape.

Aqua Designers

Takashi Amano

"Amano Takashi was born in Niigata-shi (Former Maki-machi), Niigata, Japan in 1954. He has incorporated ecosystem and other elements found in nature to aquatic plant layout and established his own unique style called “Nature Aquarium”. In 1982, Amano founded Aqua Design Amano. The company attracted much attention for its aquarium equipment “Nature Aquarium Goods” featuring excellent quality and design." -Aqua Design Amano

James Findley

"James Findley is the UK’s leading aquascaper and founder of The Green Machine. Before opening The Green Machine James was a professional fine art photographer during which time he discovered the Nature Aquarium, which represented a new and welcome challenge for his abilities. James’ artistic eye and appreciation of Nature was easily applied to this new art form. James has been working with CO2 injection in the aquarium for almost 20 years and is a true expert... Since opening The Green Machine James has pioneered new styles of aquascaping and become internationally recognised for his artistic talent and technical skill." -The Green Machine

Oliver Knott

"Oliver Knott [is] an internationally renowned live aquascaper and German aquarium designer... As a life-long lover of pet stores, Knott has built his knowledge through first-hand experience. He began as a teenager working in local pet retail stores and gradually built his own company that concentrated on setting up aquariums for small businesses and clients. Through these experiences, Knott has collected years of knowledge that has lead to expertise in the design of healthy fish tanks. His current business as a live aquascaper brings him around the globe to showcase and inspire people about how to plant beautiful tropical fresh water aquariums." -Aquatic Experience
